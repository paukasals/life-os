## 2. THE CONCEPT

**Lobsteria** — Premium seafood food truck in Miami operating from a vintage 1977 Airstream trailer converted into a professional kitchen.

**Specialties:**
- Maine lobster rolls (Connecticut warm + Maine cold)
- Oysters (5 preparations)
- Ceviches (Peruvian + Nikkei)
- Caviar add-ons (on everything)
- Event boxes / catering

**Location:** 144 NE 27th St, Miami, FL 33137 (Midtown/Wynwood)
**Hours:** Daily 5pm–2am
**Format:** Walk-in only. Outdoor seating, green turf, wooden tables, lanterns, free parking.

**The goal:** Become the recognizable Miami seafood/lobster reference point.

## 5. VISUAL IDENTITY

### Core visual world:
- Vintage Airstream stainless steel
- Warm amber lighting
- Polished metal reflections
- Real food textures
- Premium editorial food photography
- Dark navy / black / gold / warm amber accents
- Lobster red as a controlled accent
- Clean, strong typography
- Hand-crafted but refined feel

### Color palette:
- Deep navy blue (primary)
- Warm orange / lobster red (accent)
- Cream / off-white (secondary background)
- Gold (sparingly — caviar, premium moments)

### Logo:
- Vintage nautical badge style
- Script lettering
- Lobster claws + oyster shell + gold stars
- "Est. 2023" appears in some designs
- "Lobster Rolls & Good Vibes" in some identity elements

### Food photography direction:
The ideal Lobsteria food photo:
- Shot inside a vintage Airstream kitchen
- Warm amber light from top-left
- Food is the brightest element
- Stainless steel: polished, clean, reflective, but not mirror-like
- Dramatic depth, but not too dark
- Realistic shadows, real food texture
- Not overprocessed, not AI-looking
- Premium but authentic

**Key principle:** Food realism = less fake texture, more correct light behavior.

### Lobster meat specifically:
- NO snake-skin texture
- NO micro-patterning
- NO fake pixel grain
- NO metallic surface contamination
- Should look soft, fresh, slightly glossy, biological
- Texture implied by light, not drawn on the surface
- Highlights follow form and moisture, not random dots

### Bread:
- Golden toasted brioche
- Natural soft bun texture
- Not overly blistered, not plastic
- Slight butter sheen, realistic browning

### Stainless steel:
- Brushed metal grain
- Warm amber reflections
- Clean, polished, Airstream identity
- Rivets/wall details subtly visible when appropriate
- Steel texture should NEVER bleed into the food

### Key design instruction phrases (use exactly):
- "Keep exact angle and composition"
- "No reinterpretation"
- "Only change the filling"
- "Do not change structure"
- "Remove micro-patterning"
- "Food realism = less texture, more light behavior"
- "Texture is implied by light, not drawn on the surface"
- "Material separation — steel stays steel, food stays food"

## 6. THE FULL MENU

### Lobster Rolls

| Connecticut Roll | Warm brown butter, Maine lobster claw & knuckle, fresh chives, toasted brioche | $30–$42 |

| Surf & Turf Roll | 48-hour Angus short rib + browned butter Maine lobster | $40 |
| Caviar Roll | Crème fraîche + Caspian Sea sturgeon caviar | from $35 |

**Portion sizes:** 4.2 oz / 6 oz
**Bun:** New England split-style, toasted/griddled in European cultured butter (82–84% fat)

### Connecticut Warm Roll — Visual Identity:
- Warm, butter-poached / butter-tossed
- Glossy from brown butter — NOT creamy, NOT mayo-based
- Lobster pieces unified by butter, warm golden sheen
- Fresh chives on top
- AVOID: creamy mayo coating, cold salad look, dry chunks, pale look

### Maine Cold Roll — Visual Identity:
- Cold/chilled, fresh, lightly creamy
- Black garlic Old Bay mayo — slight coating, NOT heavy
- Old Bay dust visible (reddish-orange)
- Chives on top, small capers, finely diced onion/pickle
- AVOID: too much mayo, fake rubbery lobster, warm butter look

### Oysters
5 preparations — caviar upgrade available on each:
- Raw Half Shell
- Rockefeller
- Garlic Butter
- Passion Fruit (passion fruit, habanero oil, sesame)
- Leche de Tigre (lime, ají limo, cilantro)

**Pricing:** From $14 · Formats: 4 / 8 / 12 pieces

### Ceviches
- **Classic de la Tía Tati** — traditional leche de tigre, onion, ají limo, cilantro, corvina or prawns (Pau's best friend's grandmother's Peruvian recipe)
- **Tropical Passion Fruit & Mango**
- **Nikkei** — sushi-grade salmon or yellowfin tuna, soy-lime leche de tigre

**Proteins:** Corvina (premium), prawns (whole, hand-cleaned), mixto
Use "prawns" — not "shrimp" — in refined menu contexts

### Caviar Add-On
Available on EVERY item — any roll, oyster, ceviche, toast
- 10g: $30
- 20g: $55
- 30g: $75

Food cost ~$1/gram. High-margin upsell.

### Sourcing Standards (NO SHORTCUTS):
- Maine lobster: hand-cleaned whole, claw + knuckle only
- Prawns: whole, hand-cleaned
- Ceviche fish: Corvina — not cheap white fish
- Salmon + tuna: sushi-grade Grade A
- Short rib: 48-hour Angus slow-braised
- Oysters: cold-water North American, shucked to order
- Caviar: Caspian Sea sturgeon
- Truffle: real white truffle
