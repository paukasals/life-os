## STEP 2 — HOW TIKTOK IS DIFFERENT FROM INSTAGRAM

| | Instagram | TikTok |
|-|-----------|--------|
| Tone | Premium, editorial, confident | Raw, real, behind-the-scenes |
| Format | Polished photo + considered caption | Fast, handheld, uncut |
| Length | Reels: 15–30s | 15–60s (sweet spot: 20–35s) |
| Hook | Visual quality carries it | First 2 seconds must grab or they scroll |
| Audio | Brand music or silence | Trending audio OR satisfying sound design |
| Text | Minimal overlays | Text hooks on screen drive retention |
| Goal | Reinforce brand, reach Miami locals | Reach anyone — one viral video = national |
| Feel | "This is a premium brand" | "I need to show someone this right now" |

**The Lobsteria TikTok voice:**
No talking head. No vlog. No explaining. Show the process. Let the food and the sound do the work. The Airstream is the set. Pau is the character. The lobster is the star.

## STEP 3 — CONTENT STRATEGY

### The 3 TikTok Content Modes

**Mode 1: PROCESS** *(highest potential reach)*
Show the craft. Hand-cleaning lobster. Caviar being spooned. Butter pouring. Oyster shucked in one motion. These are satisfying, shareable, and unique. Nobody else in Miami is showing this.

**Mode 2: CONTRAST** *(highest shareability)*
Caviar on a food truck. $75 add-on served through an Airstream window. White truffle on the street at 1am. The visual tension between "luxury" and "walk-up" is the hook. Show it without explaining it.

**Mode 3: LATE NIGHT** *(highest local conversion)*
The Airstream at midnight. The line. The lanterns. Plates going out. Miami at 1am. This converts locals who are out — they see it and come. Timestamp matters: post these at 9pm–11pm when people are deciding where to go.

## STEP 4 — FIRST 3 VIDEOS (ready to shoot)

---

### VIDEO 1: Hand-Cleaning Lobster
**Theme:** Process · Trust · "This is why it's different"
**Target:** Foodies, food lovers anywhere, viral potential

**Shot list:**
- Open on whole live/fresh Maine lobster on stainless steel
- Close-up hands cracking, removing shell
- Pull out claw meat in one clean piece — slow and satisfying
- Cut knuckle meat — show the yield
- Drop into bowl — glistening, fresh
- Final frame: finished Connecticut Roll going out the window

**Audio:** Natural sound only — cracking, steel, ambient kitchen noise. Or trending "satisfying food" audio.

**On-screen text (appear sequentially):**
> "This is a Maine lobster."
> "Claw and knuckle only."
> "Hand-cleaned. Every night."
> "This is why it takes 15 minutes."

**Caption:** `This is why it takes 15 minutes. #lobsterroll #miami #mainelobster #foodtok`

**Length:** 25–30 seconds
**Best post time:** Monday 7pm
